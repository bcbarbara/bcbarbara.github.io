Crazyflie

The Crazyflie 2.1 nano quadcopter is my current project as a visting PhD student in the University of Freiburg, Germany. I've been under the supervision of professor Moritz Diehl at the Systems Control and Optimization Laboratory (syscop), and supported by the Autonomous Intelligent Systems Lab of professor Wolfram Burgard and Kiteswarms.

NMPC for trajectory generation and tracking

The project is about trajectory generation and tracking using NMPC for collision avoidance with dynamic obstacles. It consists in an efficient and modular implementation of a Nonlinear Model Predictive Control (NMPC) tailored for the Crazyflie's online trajectory generation and tracking problem. A Real-Time Iteration (RTI) scheme through a Sequential Quadratic Programming (SQP) online algorithm is used in order to solve the Nonlinear Program (NLP). Initially the problem is solved offline using a primal-dual interior-point method solver with CasADi, and then a high-performance interior-point method solver is used for the online calculations through acados interface.

The tests are performed in the Autonomous Intelligent Systems arena which has ten Raptor-E cameras at 100 Hz used to record datasets for the experiments. On the other hand, the crazyflie counts with:

  • IMU.
  • Optical flow camera.
  • Miniature autopilot.
  • Bluetooth LE.
  • Low-latency/long-range radio.
